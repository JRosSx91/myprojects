Oskar Schindler

The German who saved 1200 Jews

Key Facts

Oskar Schindler was a German businessman and a member of the Nazi Party. In November 1939, he acquired some factories in German-occupied Poland by taking advantage of the German policy to "Aryanize" and "Germanize" Jewish-owned and Polish-owned businesses.

The best known of these factories today is the “Emalia” factory in Kraków. Before Schindler acquired this factory, it had been Jewish-owned. At Emalia, Schindler employed Jewish forced laborers from the Krakau-Plaszow camp.

By employing Jews and protecting them from the systematic brutality and arbitrary murder of daily life in a concentration camp, Schindler facilitated the survival of over 1,000 Jews. He was posthumously given the title "Righteous Among the Nations" in 1993.
